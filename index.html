My Reflection

Theme 1 (Social): Classism really split the U S into two different worlds during this time. On one side, you had the super rich who lived in massive palaces. They liked to use ideas like the "Gospel of Wealth" to say that they were just "the fittest" and that having all the money was actually good for the country. But Muckrakers like Jacob Riis showed a totally different story. People were living in tiny, gross rooms with no windows or fresh air. It wasn't that they were lazy bc they were working 14 hours a day and still couldn't afford a decent place to live. This made everyone realize that the "American Dream" wasn't working for the regular people doing all the hard work.

Theme 2 (Politics): The government used to only care about the rich and ignored everyone else. Big companies did whatever they wanted, and they didn't care if workers got hurt. But the public went crazy once the Muckrakers exposed the kids in coal mines and the gross food factories. They forced the government to finally do something. That’s how we got the first laws to stop child labor and make jobs safer. The government finally had to stand up to the rich.

Annotated Works Cited

  • Carnegie, Andrew. "The Gospel of Wealth." (1889).

    I used this to show the "Yellow Journalism" side and how the rich made excuses for why they had all the money while others had none.

  • Riis, Jacob. "How the Other Half Lives." (1890).

    This provided the actual proof and photos of how bad people were living in the slums and tenements.

  • Sinclair, Upton. "The Jungle." (1906).

    This helped me describe the dangerous conditions in factories and why the government had to pass new safety laws.

  • Tarbell, Ida. "The History of the Standard Oil Company." (1904).

    This helped me understand how monopolies worked and how big companies crushed regular workers to get more power.
